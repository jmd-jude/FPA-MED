The evaluation included a review of extensive collateral documentation (over 5,000 pages), including police reports, victim statements, pre-sentence investigation reports (PSIRs), institutional disciplinary records, treatment progress notes, and previous psychological evaluations. Three structured clinical interviews were conducted.

### IV. Likelihood of Future Predatory Sexual Violence

Based on the synthesis of data, the following conclusions are reached:
1.  **Mental Abnormality:** Mr. Vance suffers from a serious mental abnormality, specifically **Paraphilic Disorder, Other Specified (Sexual Sadism)**, and a **Personality Disorder (Antisocial Personality Disorder)**. The paraphilia is deemed the core mechanism driving his predatory behavior.
2.  **Likelihood of Re-offense:** The risk assessment instruments unanimously place Mr. Vance in the **High-Risk** category for future sexual recidivism. The absence of effective treatment, the severity of his underlying psychopathy (PCL-R 35), the chronic nature of his paraphilic behavior, and his lack of genuine insight or remorse are dynamic factors that, in combination with his extensive static risk (Static-99R 8), make re-offense highly likely.
3.  **Conclusion:** Mr. Vance meets the statutory criteria for commitment as a Sexually Violent Predator. His mental abnormality and personality disorder render him a menace to the health and safety of others because it is **likely** that he will engage in predatory acts of sexual violence.
