CLINICAL INTERVIEW FINDINGS:
Mr. Anderson was cooperative during the evaluation. He demonstrated adequate orientation to person, place, and time. His speech was coherent and goal-directed. He was able to provide a detailed account of his legal situation and demonstrated understanding of the charges against him.

Mental status examination revealed no evidence of acute psychosis. Mr. Anderson denied current hallucinations or delusions. His mood was described as "anxious but stable." His thought processes were logical and sequential. There were no signs of formal thought disorder.

PSYCHIATRIC HISTORY:
Mr. Anderson reported a history of major depressive disorder, first diagnosed in 2015. He has been treated with sertraline 100mg daily for the past three years with good symptom control. He denies any history of psychiatric hospitalization. There is no reported history of suicide attempts or self-harm behaviors.

He denies any history of substance abuse. Collateral information from medical records confirms his reported psychiatric history and medication compliance.

COMPETENCY ASSESSMENT:
Mr. Anderson demonstrates adequate understanding of the legal proceedings. He can identify the roles of key courtroom personnel including the judge, prosecutor, defense attorney, and jury. He understands the charges against him and the potential consequences of conviction.

He can rationally consult with his attorney and assist in his own defense. He demonstrates the ability to understand and weigh legal options, including the possibility of plea negotiations. His decision-making capacity appears intact.

MENTAL STATE AT TIME OF OFFENSE:
Mr. Anderson provides a detailed and coherent account of events surrounding the alleged offense. There is no evidence that his judgment or behavior was significantly impaired by mental illness at the time. His account is internally consistent and aligns with available collateral information.

DIAGNOSTIC IMPRESSIONS:
1. Major Depressive Disorder, in remission (on medication)
2. No acute mental illness identified

OPINIONS:
It is my professional opinion that Mr. Anderson is competent to stand trial. He possesses both factual and rational understanding of the legal proceedings and can effectively assist his attorney in his defense.

Regarding his mental state at the time of the alleged offense, there is no evidence that Mr. Anderson was suffering from a mental disease or defect that would have substantially impaired his capacity to appreciate the wrongfulness of his conduct or to conform his conduct to the requirements of law.

RECOMMENDATIONS:
1. Mr. Anderson should continue his current psychiatric medication regimen
2. No inpatient psychiatric treatment is indicated at this time
3. He is competent to proceed with legal proceedings
